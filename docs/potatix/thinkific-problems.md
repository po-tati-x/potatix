## currrent issues with thinkific

* **Cumbersome bulk enrollment** – CSV upload required *per course*, only one upload can run at a time, each takes 10-20 minutes; enrolling 15 000 students took two full days.
* **Primitive affiliate platform** – No bulk affiliate import, no self-service signup, every commission must be approved manually; affiliates can’t track clicks well, leading to a 70 % drop in partner revenue.
* **Out-of-date video player** – No automatic captioning; SRT files must be uploaded one-by-one.
* **Incomplete subscription logic** – Platform adds a “member” tag when a user subscribes but never removes it on cancellation, forcing users to pay for Zapier zaps; Thinkific also resists transferring active Stripe subscriptions when you migrate away.
* **Ineffective revenue-share tool** – Not tied to instructor accounts or dashboards, so partners can’t see or verify earnings.
* **Weak website builder** – Templates are rigid; serious design work requires costly custom code or third-party page-builder apps.
* **Sparse product updates & generic support** – Six months passed without meaningful feature releases; support replies link to docs rather than investigating issues, even after multiple escalations.
* **“Coming soon” email marketing** – A menu item appears in the dashboard but is still non-functional, suggesting marketing over substance.
* **Minimal upsell / order-bump flow** – Upsell copy limited to a few characters, rendered as a tiny button many students never notice.
* **Disjointed community module** – Feels bolted-on and separate from the core course experience.
* **Slow overall performance** – Navigation and admin actions drag noticeably, hurting day-to-day workflow.
* **Misleading all-in-one promise** – Key sales, marketing, email, and analytics capabilities are either missing or so limited that experienced creators must bolt on extra software, erasing the cost advantage.
